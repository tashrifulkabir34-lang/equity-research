## Investment Thesis — Visa Inc. (NYSE: V)

The market is mispricing Visa because it is treating regulatory noise
on interchange fees as an existential threat, while missing that Visa
is quietly transforming into a commerce infrastructure company.

Value-Added Services grew 28% YoY in Q1 FY2026 and Visa Direct
processed 12.5 billion transactions in 2025 — eightfold growth since
2019. The toll-road is becoming a platform.

At 28.3x TTM earnings — 16% below Visa's own 10-year average P/E of
33.6x and at a discount to peer Mastercard (31.1x) — this mispricing
creates a compelling entry point with ~19% upside to a conservative
base case target of $358.

## Methodology

Each memo follows a disciplined 1-page format built around one
central question: what does the market have wrong, and why?

- One clear edge sentence — the entire thesis in 2-3 sentences
- Three reasons to own — each anchored to a specific data point
- Plain English business description — no jargon
- Bear / Base / Bull valuation scenarios with explicit assumptions
- Honest risk acknowledgment and one near-term catalyst
